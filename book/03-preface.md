# Preface {.unnumbered}

This book accompanies the **Tesla Engine** interactive physics simulation project, a web-based
platform designed to make classical mechanics accessible through hands-on experimentation.

The four topics covered here — *Uniform Motion*, *Uniformly Accelerated Motion*, *Free Fall*,
and *Projectile Motion* — represent the foundational building blocks of classical kinematics.
Understanding them gives a solid base for exploring all other areas of Newtonian mechanics.

Each chapter pairs the underlying mathematical theory with practical examples and guidance
for using the online simulations. The reader is encouraged to visit the live demos and
experiment with parameters while reading.

**How to read this book**

- Chapter 1 covers Uniform Motion: what it is, the governing equations, and worked examples.
- Chapter 2 covers Uniformly Accelerated Motion: constant acceleration from rest, the
  kinematic equations, and how position grows quadratically with time.
- Chapter 3 covers Free Fall: Galileo's experimental result that all objects fall with the
  same acceleration g regardless of mass, and the role of air resistance.
- Chapter 4 covers Projectile Motion: decomposition into horizontal and vertical components,
  key formulas, and simulation guidance.

No advanced mathematics is required beyond basic algebra and trigonometry.
