# ✅ Uniform Circular Motion — Solutions
{: .section-heading }

Full step-by-step solutions to all 20 problems from the [Exercises page]({{ '/uniform-circular-motion-exercises/' | relative_url }}). All calculations use \\(g = 9.81 \, \text{m/s}^2\\) and radians throughout.

## ⭐ Level 1 — Warm-up
{: .section-heading }

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**1.**</span> \\(\theta = 60°\\).

\\[\theta_{\text{rad}} = 60° \times \frac{\pi}{180} = \frac{\pi}{3} \approx 1.047 \, \text{rad}\\]

<span class="final-answer">θ = π/3 ≈ 1.047 rad</span>
</div>

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**2.**</span> \\(\theta = \dfrac{3\pi}{2} \, \text{rad}\\).

\\[\theta_{\text{deg}} = \frac{3\pi}{2} \times \frac{180}{\pi} = 3 \times 90° = 270°\\]

<span class="final-answer">θ = 270°</span>
</div>

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**3.**</span> \\(T = 2 \, \text{s}\\).

\\[\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T} = \frac{2\pi}{2} = \pi \approx 3.14 \, \text{rad/s}\\]

<span class="final-answer">ω = π ≈ 3.14 rad/s</span>
</div>

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**4.**</span> \\(\omega = 4 \, \text{rad/s}\\), \\(r = 2 \, \text{m}\\).

\\[v = \omega \cdot r = 4 \times 2 = 8 \, \text{m/s}\\]

<span class="final-answer">v = 8 m/s</span>
</div>

## ⭐⭐ Level 2 — Getting Comfortable
{: .section-heading }

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**5.**</span> \\(r = 7000 \, \text{km} = 7 \times 10^6 \, \text{m}\\), \\(v = 7.5 \, \text{km/s} = 7500 \, \text{m/s}\\).

\\[\omega = \frac{v}{r} = \frac{7500}{7 \times 10^6} \approx 1.071 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{rad/s}\\]
\\[T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega} = \frac{6.283}{1.071 \times 10^{-3}} \approx 5864.6 \, \text{s} = \frac{5864.6}{60} \approx 97.7 \, \text{min}\\]

<span class="final-answer">ω ≈ 1.07 × 10⁻³ rad/s, T ≈ 97.7 min</span>
</div>

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**6.**</span> \\(r = 0.5 \, \text{m}\\), \\(\omega = 10 \, \text{rad/s}\\).

\\[a_c = \omega^2 r = 10^2 \times 0.5 = 100 \times 0.5\\]

<span class="final-answer">a_c = 50 m/s²</span>
</div>

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**7.**</span> \\(T = 0.02 \, \text{s}\\).

\\[f = \frac{1}{T} = \frac{1}{0.02} = 50 \, \text{Hz}\\]
\\[\omega = 2\pi f = 2\pi \times 50 \approx 314.2 \, \text{rad/s}\\]

<span class="final-answer">f = 50 Hz, ω ≈ 314.2 rad/s</span>
</div>

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**8.**</span> \\(f = 50 \, \text{Hz}\\), \\(r = 0.1 \, \text{m}\\).

\\[\omega = 2\pi f \approx 314.2 \, \text{rad/s}\\]
\\[v = \omega r = 314.2 \times 0.1 \approx 31.4 \, \text{m/s}\\]
\\[a_c = \omega^2 r = 314.2^2 \times 0.1 \approx 98696 \times 0.1\\]

<span class="final-answer">v ≈ 31.4 m/s, a_c ≈ 9870 m/s²</span>
</div>

## ⭐⭐⭐ Level 3 — Building Skills
{: .section-heading }

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**9.**</span> \\(r = 3 \, \text{m}\\), \\(\theta_0 = 0\\), \\(\omega = 2 \, \text{rad/s}\\), \\(t = 1 \, \text{s}\\).

\\[\theta = \theta_0 + \omega t = 0 + 2 \times 1 = 2 \, \text{rad}\\]
\\[x = r\cos\theta = 3\cos(2) = 3 \times (-0.416) \approx -1.25 \, \text{m}\\]
\\[y = r\sin\theta = 3\sin(2) = 3 \times 0.909 \approx 2.73 \, \text{m}\\]

<span class="final-answer">(x, y) ≈ (−1.25, 2.73) m</span>
</div>

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**10.**</span> Same setup as Exercise 9, \\(\theta = 2 \, \text{rad}\\).

\\[v_x = -r\omega\sin\theta = -3 \times 2 \times 0.909 \approx -5.46 \, \text{m/s}\\]
\\[v_y = r\omega\cos\theta = 3 \times 2 \times (-0.416) \approx -2.50 \, \text{m/s}\\]
\\[|v| = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2} = \sqrt{5.46^2 + 2.50^2} \approx 6.00 \, \text{m/s}\\]

This matches \\(v = \omega r = 2 \times 3 = 6 \, \text{m/s}\\) exactly, confirming the vector components are consistent with the scalar formula.

<span class="final-answer">(v_x, v_y) ≈ (−5.46, −2.50) m/s, |v| = 6.00 m/s</span>
</div>

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**11.**</span> \\(r = 50 \, \text{m}\\), \\(v = 20 \, \text{m/s}\\).

\\[a_c = \frac{v^2}{r} = \frac{400}{50} = 8 \, \text{m/s}^2\\]
\\[\frac{a_c}{g} = \frac{8}{9.81} \approx 0.82\\]

<span class="final-answer">a_c = 8 m/s² ≈ 0.82g</span>
</div>

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**12.**</span> \\(r = 10 \, \text{cm} = 0.1 \, \text{m}\\), \\(T = 60 \, \text{s}\\) (one revolution per minute).

\\[\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T} = \frac{2\pi}{60} \approx 0.1047 \, \text{rad/s}\\]
\\[v = \omega r = 0.1047 \times 0.1 \approx 0.0105 \, \text{m/s} \approx 1.05 \, \text{cm/s}\\]
\\[a_c = \omega^2 r = 0.1047^2 \times 0.1 \approx 1.10 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{m/s}^2\\]

<span class="final-answer">ω ≈ 0.105 rad/s, v ≈ 1.05 cm/s, a_c ≈ 1.10 × 10⁻³ m/s²</span>
</div>

## ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Level 4 — Challenge
{: .section-heading }

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**13.**</span> \\(1200 \, \text{rpm}\\), \\(r = 0.3 \, \text{m}\\).

\\[\omega = 1200 \times \frac{2\pi}{60} = 1200 \times 0.1047 \approx 125.7 \, \text{rad/s}\\]
\\[v = \omega r = 125.7 \times 0.3 \approx 37.7 \, \text{m/s}\\]
\\[a_c = \omega^2 r = 125.7^2 \times 0.3 \approx 15791 \times 0.3\\]

<span class="final-answer">ω ≈ 125.7 rad/s, v ≈ 37.7 m/s, a_c ≈ 4737 m/s²</span>
</div>

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**14.**</span> \\(a_c = 50 \, \text{m/s}^2\\), \\(r = 2 \, \text{m}\\).

\\[\omega = \sqrt{\frac{a_c}{r}} = \sqrt{\frac{50}{2}} = \sqrt{25} = 5 \, \text{rad/s}\\]
\\[v = \omega r = 5 \times 2 = 10 \, \text{m/s}\\]

<span class="final-answer">ω = 5 rad/s, v = 10 m/s</span>
</div>

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**15.**</span> \\(r = 0.4 \, \text{m}\\), 10 revolutions in \\(t = 5 \, \text{s}\\).

\\[\Delta\theta = 10 \times 2\pi \approx 62.83 \, \text{rad} \implies \omega = \frac{\Delta\theta}{t} = \frac{62.83}{5} \approx 12.57 \, \text{rad/s}\\]
\\[T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega} \approx 0.5 \, \text{s} \quad \text{(matches 10 rev / 5 s = 2 rev/s)}\\]
\\[f = \frac{1}{T} = 2 \, \text{Hz}\\]
\\[v = \omega r = 12.57 \times 0.4 \approx 5.03 \, \text{m/s}\\]
\\[a_c = \omega^2 r = 12.57^2 \times 0.4 \approx 157.9 \times 0.4\\]

<span class="final-answer">ω ≈ 12.57 rad/s, T = 0.5 s, f = 2 Hz, v ≈ 5.03 m/s, a_c ≈ 63.2 m/s²</span>
</div>

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**16.**</span> \\(\omega = 6 \, \text{rad/s}\\) (shared), \\(r_1 = 0.2 \, \text{m}\\), \\(r_2 = 0.5 \, \text{m}\\).

\\[v_1 = \omega r_1 = 6 \times 0.2 = 1.2 \, \text{m/s}, \quad v_2 = \omega r_2 = 6 \times 0.5 = 3.0 \, \text{m/s}\\]
\\[a_{c1} = \omega^2 r_1 = 36 \times 0.2 = 7.2 \, \text{m/s}^2, \quad a_{c2} = \omega^2 r_2 = 36 \times 0.5 = 18 \, \text{m/s}^2\\]

Both ratios equal \\(r_2 / r_1\\), since both v and \\(a_c\\) are directly proportional to r at fixed ω:

\\[\frac{v_2}{v_1} = \frac{a_{c2}}{a_{c1}} = \frac{0.5}{0.2} = 2.5\\]

<span class="final-answer">v₁ = 1.2 m/s, v₂ = 3.0 m/s, a_c1 = 7.2 m/s², a_c2 = 18 m/s² — both ratios = 2.5</span>
</div>

## ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Level 5 — Expert
{: .section-heading }

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**17.**</span> \\(a_c = 5000g = 5000 \times 9.81 = 49050 \, \text{m/s}^2\\), \\(r = 0.15 \, \text{m}\\).

\\[\omega = \sqrt{\frac{a_c}{r}} = \sqrt{\frac{49050}{0.15}} = \sqrt{327000} \approx 571.8 \, \text{rad/s}\\]
\\[\text{rpm} = \omega \times \frac{60}{2\pi} = 571.8 \times 9.549 \approx 5461\\]

<span class="final-answer">ω ≈ 571.8 rad/s ≈ 5461 rpm</span>
</div>

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**18.**</span> \\(r = 4 \, \text{m}\\), \\(\theta_0 = 30° = \pi/6 \, \text{rad}\\), \\(\omega = 3 \, \text{rad/s}\\), \\(t = 2 \, \text{s}\\).

\\[\theta = \theta_0 + \omega t = 0.5236 + 6 = 6.5236 \, \text{rad}\\]

Subtracting one full turn (\\(2\pi \approx 6.2832\\)) to bring it into range:

\\[\theta \equiv 6.5236 - 6.2832 = 0.2404 \, \text{rad} \, (\approx 13.8°)\\]
\\[x = r\cos\theta = 4 \times 0.971 \approx 3.89 \, \text{m}, \quad y = r\sin\theta = 4 \times 0.238 \approx 0.95 \, \text{m}\\]
\\[v_x = -r\omega\sin\theta = -12 \times 0.238 \approx -2.86 \, \text{m/s}\\]
\\[v_y = r\omega\cos\theta = 12 \times 0.971 \approx 11.65 \, \text{m/s}\\]

As a check, \\(|v| = \sqrt{v_x^2+v_y^2} \approx 12.0 \, \text{m/s} = \omega r\\). ✓

<span class="final-answer">(x, y) ≈ (3.89, 0.95) m, (v_x, v_y) ≈ (−2.86, 11.65) m/s</span>
</div>

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**19.**</span> \\(T = 24 \, \text{h} = 86400 \, \text{s}\\), \\(r = 42164 \, \text{km} = 4.2164 \times 10^7 \, \text{m}\\).

\\[\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T} = \frac{6.283}{86400} \approx 7.272 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{rad/s}\\]
\\[v = \omega r \approx 7.272 \times 10^{-5} \times 4.2164 \times 10^7 \approx 3066 \, \text{m/s} \approx 3.07 \, \text{km/s}\\]
\\[a_c = \omega^2 r \approx (7.272 \times 10^{-5})^2 \times 4.2164 \times 10^7 \approx 0.223 \, \text{m/s}^2\\]

<span class="final-answer">ω ≈ 7.27 × 10⁻⁵ rad/s, v ≈ 3.07 km/s, a_c ≈ 0.223 m/s²</span>
</div>

<div class="solution-item" markdown="1">
<span class="sol-num">**20.**</span> \\(a_c = 4g = 4 \times 9.81 = 39.24 \, \text{m/s}^2\\), \\(L = 0.8 \, \text{m}\\).

\\[v = \sqrt{a_c L} = \sqrt{39.24 \times 0.8} = \sqrt{31.39} \approx 5.60 \, \text{m/s}\\]
\\[\omega = \sqrt{\frac{a_c}{L}} = \sqrt{\frac{39.24}{0.8}} = \sqrt{49.05} \approx 7.00 \, \text{rad/s}\\]
\\[T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega} \approx \frac{6.283}{7.00} \approx 0.897 \, \text{s}\\]
\\[\text{rpm} = \frac{60}{T} \approx \frac{60}{0.897} \approx 66.9\\]

<span class="final-answer">v ≈ 5.60 m/s, ω ≈ 7.00 rad/s, T ≈ 0.897 s ≈ 66.9 rpm</span>
</div>
