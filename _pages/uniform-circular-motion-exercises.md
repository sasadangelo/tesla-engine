# 📝 Uniform Circular Motion — Exercises
{: .section-heading }

Twenty problems to practice the equations from the [Uniform Circular Motion Theory]({{ '/uniform-circular-motion-theory/' | relative_url }}) and [Degrees vs. Radians Math JIT]({{ '/uniform-circular-motion-math-jit/' | relative_url }}) pages, arranged in five levels of increasing difficulty — from ⭐ (warm-up) to ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (expert). Unless stated otherwise, use \\(g = 9.81 \, \text{m/s}^2\\) and remember: **ω must be in rad/s** for the circular-motion formulas to work.
{: .theory-section }

> ✅ **Check your work**
> Every problem below is fully solved, step by step, on the [Solutions page]({{ '/uniform-circular-motion-solutions/' | relative_url }}). Try to solve each exercise on your own first!
{: .math-jit-box }

---

## ⭐ Level 1 — Warm-up
{: .section-heading }

<div class="exercise-item tier-1" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**1.**</span> Convert \\(\theta = 60°\\) to radians.
</div>

<div class="exercise-item tier-1" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**2.**</span> Convert \\(\theta = \dfrac{3\pi}{2} \, \text{rad}\\) to degrees.
</div>

<div class="exercise-item tier-1" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**3.**</span> A wheel completes one full revolution every \\(T = 2 \, \text{s}\\). Find its angular velocity \\(\omega\\).
</div>

<div class="exercise-item tier-1" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**4.**</span> A point on a merry-go-round moves with \\(\omega = 4 \, \text{rad/s}\\) along a circle of radius \\(r = 2 \, \text{m}\\). Find its linear speed \\(v\\).
</div>

---

## ⭐⭐ Level 2 — Getting Comfortable
{: .section-heading }

<div class="exercise-item tier-2" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**5.**</span> A satellite orbits at radius \\(r = 7000 \, \text{km}\\) with constant speed \\(v = 7.5 \, \text{km/s}\\). Find its angular velocity \\(\omega\\) and its orbital period \\(T\\) (in minutes).
</div>

<div class="exercise-item tier-2" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**6.**</span> A point moves on a circle of radius \\(r = 0.5 \, \text{m}\\) with \\(\omega = 10 \, \text{rad/s}\\). Find the centripetal acceleration \\(a_c\\).
</div>

<div class="exercise-item tier-2" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**7.**</span> A spinning disk completes one revolution every \\(T = 0.02 \, \text{s}\\). Find its frequency \\(f\\) and angular velocity \\(\omega\\).
</div>

<div class="exercise-item tier-2" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**8.**</span> An AC motor spins at \\(f = 50 \, \text{Hz}\\). A point on its shaft sits at \\(r = 0.1 \, \text{m}\\) from the axis. Find the linear speed \\(v\\) and centripetal acceleration \\(a_c\\) of that point.
</div>

---

## ⭐⭐⭐ Level 3 — Building Skills
{: .section-heading }

<div class="exercise-item tier-3" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**9.**</span> A body moves on a circle of radius \\(r = 3 \, \text{m}\\) centered at the origin, starting at \\(\theta_0 = 0\\) with \\(\omega = 2 \, \text{rad/s}\\). Find its position \\((x, y)\\) at \\(t = 1 \, \text{s}\\).
</div>

<div class="exercise-item tier-3" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**10.**</span> For the same body as Exercise 9, find the velocity vector \\((v_x, v_y)\\) at \\(t = 1 \, \text{s}\\), and verify its magnitude matches \\(v = \omega r\\).
</div>

<div class="exercise-item tier-3" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**11.**</span> A car rounds a circular curve of radius \\(r = 50 \, \text{m}\\) at a constant \\(v = 20 \, \text{m/s}\\). Find the centripetal acceleration and express it as a multiple of \\(g\\).
</div>

<div class="exercise-item tier-3" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**12.**</span> The second hand of a clock is \\(10 \, \text{cm}\\) long. Find its angular velocity \\(\omega\\), the linear speed of its tip, and the centripetal acceleration of its tip.
</div>

---

## ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Level 4 — Challenge
{: .section-heading }

<div class="exercise-item tier-4" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**13.**</span> A fan blade tip is at radius \\(r = 0.3 \, \text{m}\\) and spins at \\(1200 \, \text{rpm}\\) (revolutions per minute). Convert this to \\(\omega\\) in rad/s, then find the tip's linear speed and centripetal acceleration.
</div>

<div class="exercise-item tier-4" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**14.**</span> A point undergoes centripetal acceleration \\(a_c = 50 \, \text{m/s}^2\\) at radius \\(r = 2 \, \text{m}\\). Find \\(\omega\\) and \\(v\\).
</div>

<div class="exercise-item tier-4" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**15.**</span> A wheel of radius \\(r = 0.4 \, \text{m}\\) completes 10 revolutions in \\(t = 5 \, \text{s}\\). Find \\(\omega\\), \\(T\\), \\(f\\), the rim speed \\(v\\), and the centripetal acceleration \\(a_c\\) at the rim.
</div>

<div class="exercise-item tier-4" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**16.**</span> Two points sit on the same rotating disk (same \\(\omega = 6 \, \text{rad/s}\\)) at \\(r_1 = 0.2 \, \text{m}\\) and \\(r_2 = 0.5 \, \text{m}\\). Compare their linear speeds and centripetal accelerations — find the ratio in each case.
</div>

---

## ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Level 5 — Expert
{: .section-heading }

<div class="exercise-item tier-5" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**17.**</span> A lab centrifuge must produce a centripetal acceleration of \\(5000g\\) at radius \\(r = 0.15 \, \text{m}\\). Find the required angular velocity \\(\omega\\) (rad/s) and the equivalent speed in rpm.
</div>

<div class="exercise-item tier-5" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**18.**</span> A body moves on a circle of radius \\(r = 4 \, \text{m}\\), starting at \\(\theta_0 = 30°\\) with \\(\omega = 3 \, \text{rad/s}\\) counter-clockwise. Find its position \\((x, y)\\) and velocity vector \\((v_x, v_y)\\) at \\(t = 2 \, \text{s}\\).
</div>

<div class="exercise-item tier-5" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**19.**</span> A satellite orbits with period \\(T = 24 \, \text{h}\\) at radius \\(r = 42164 \, \text{km}\\) (a geostationary-type orbit). Find its angular velocity \\(\omega\\) (rad/s), linear speed \\(v\\) (km/s), and centripetal acceleration \\(a_c\\) (m/s²).
</div>

<div class="exercise-item tier-5" markdown="1">
<span class="ex-num">**20.**</span> A stone tied to a string of length \\(L = 0.8 \, \text{m}\\) is swung in a horizontal circle so that its centripetal acceleration equals \\(4g\\). Find the required speed \\(v\\), angular velocity \\(\omega\\), period \\(T\\), and the equivalent rotation rate in rpm.
</div>
